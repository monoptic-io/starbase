# Music Theory

**Music theory** is the grammar of organized sound. It does not tell you what to feel; it names the patterns that reliably *produce* feeling, so that a fleeting intuition — "that note wants to fall," "that chord sounds bright" — becomes something you can describe, predict, and reuse. Like any grammar it has just two raw materials: **pitch** (how high or low a sound is) and **time** (when sounds begin and end). Everything else is built by combining those two.

This section is a self-contained tour. It starts from a single tone and climbs, one idea resting on the last, until you can read why a chord progression resolves and why one key feels different from another.

## The two axes

Almost every concept here lives on one of two axes:

- **The pitch axis** — [[Pitch]] gives us tones; the gap between two tones is an [[Interval]]; an ordered ladder of intervals is a [[Scale]]; stacking scale tones makes a [[Chord]]; chords in motion make [[Harmony]].
- **The time axis** — [[Rhythm]] organizes *when* sounds happen, slicing the flow of time into beats and measures.

The two axes are independent — you can keep a melody and change its rhythm, or keep a rhythm and change its pitches — yet music only comes alive when they move together.

A small set of pitches, chosen by a [[Key Signature]] and ordered into a [[Scale]], is stacked into [[Chord|chords]] that move through time in a [[Rhythm]] to create tension and release — and the map of how those keys relate is the [[Circle of Fifths]].

## How the ideas build

1. [[Pitch]] — a single tone, and why the octave repeats every twelve steps.
2. [[Interval]] — the distance between two tones, and why some gaps sound sweet and others tense.
3. [[Scale]] — a chosen ladder of intervals; the major scale's famous W-W-H-W-W-W-H.
4. [[Chord]] — three or more tones stacked in thirds; major versus minor.
5. [[Harmony]] — chords in sequence, building and releasing tension.
6. [[Rhythm]] — organizing time into beats, meters, and tempo.
7. [[Circle of Fifths]] — the master map relating all twelve keys.
8. [[Key Signature]] — which sharps or flats a piece carries.
9. [[Mode]] — the seven moods hidden inside one scale.

## Where it connects

These ideas reinforce one another in a loop rather than a line. A [[Scale]] and a [[Key Signature]] are two views of the same set of notes; the [[Circle of Fifths]] explains *why* those notes cluster the way they do; a [[Mode]] is just that same scale started from a different rung. Read them in order the first time, then follow the cross-links wherever your curiosity pulls.
